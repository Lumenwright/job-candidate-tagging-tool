Detect whether the candidate states an EXPLICIT engineering or design tradeoff:
choosing one option over another, with what was given up or why.

FIRES WHEN the text names a concrete compromise such as:
- speed / time-to-ship vs accuracy, model size, or completeness, or
- deterministic code vs dynamic LLM generation, or
- cost or API-budget limits driving a choice, or
- latency vs quality, build vs buy, or simplicity vs flexibility, or
- accepting a known limitation to gain something (e.g. eventual consistency for
  latency), where both the choice AND its reason or cost are stated.

DOES NOT FIRE WHEN the text only:
- uses sweeping verbs with no decision ("drove success", "managed complexity",
  "navigated stakeholders", "transformed the business"), or
- says a decision was "hard" without stating what was traded off or chosen, or
- lists technologies without a comparative choice between alternatives, or
- describes a preference with no alternative considered or cost acknowledged.

Cite the exact span showing the choice and its tradeoff or rationale. Empty
leadership or impact language without a concrete decision must NOT fire.
