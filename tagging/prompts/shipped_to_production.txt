Detect whether the candidate states that something they built reached a real,
operational, in-use state — not just that it was built.

FIRES WHEN the text explicitly says a system, application, or workflow was:
- deployed to production or to real end users, or
- used in real operations / day-to-day work by real people, or
- maintained, supported, or operated after launch, or
- integrated into an active, live operational system.
Real-usage phrasing counts even if the word "production" is absent
(e.g. "used daily by 40 drivers", "rolled out to the support team").

DOES NOT FIRE WHEN the build is only:
- a prototype, proof of concept, demo, pilot-not-launched, hackathon project,
  sandbox experiment, or local script, or
- described as "built" / "developed" / "delivered" with no statement that it
  went live or was used operationally, or
- a planned or intended deployment that has not happened.

Cite the exact span that states the live/operational/maintained status. If the
only evidence is generic ("delivered solutions", "drove impact") with no
operational detail, do not fire.
